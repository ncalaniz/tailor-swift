About The Role

In this role, you will own Customer Experience Operations and Partner Operations, and provide hands-on support for Sales Operations. You will report directly to the VP of Revenue Operations & SMB Sales and partner closely with them on the broader revenue operations agenda across our go-to-market and post-sale motions.

At Ketch, operations sits at the intersection of systems, process, and data: you design systems that minimize manual process wherever reasonable, building for scale rather than automation that gets torn out in six months. Those systems capture the data needed to analyze GTM processes, and those analyses drive the next round of process and systems improvements.

We also believe AI is changing what a lean operations team can accomplish. Rather than building out separate ops leads, system admins, and pods of analysts, we are looking for one exceptional operator who works with AI to cover that ground: streamlining systems improvements and building AI-powered tools that put data and analysis directly in the hands of leaders across the business.

This is a remote role within the United States.

Operations:
Own the operational backbone for Customer Success, Professional Services, Support, and Renewals. Design and implement scalable systems that reduce manual work across onboarding, health scoring, escalations, capacity forecasting and management, services delivery, and the renewal motion. Build the analyses that surface churn risk, expansion opportunity, services margin, and support efficiency, and turn findings into process and systems changes. 
Stand up and maintain the operational infrastructure for our partner ecosystem: partner onboarding, deal registration, co-sell tracking, partner-sourced pipeline reporting, certifications, delivery quality, churn, and psat. Give partner leadership clear visibility into partner performance and program ROI. 
Support core sales operations: pipeline hygiene, forecasting cadence, process questions, and CRM workflow improvements. Partner with sales leadership on analyses that inform go-to-market decisions. 
Systems:
Design durable systems, not quick fixes. Do the math: quantify and prioritize business value before investing in automation. Ensure every system captures the data needed to analyze the process it runs. Make responsible, effective use of company assets. 
Work with AI tools to accelerate systems design, administration, and integration work that historically required dedicated admins. Build AI-driven analytical tools and self-serve reporting that empower leaders across the business to answer their own questions. Continuously evaluate where AI can replace manual processes, and where it shouldn’t. 
Data & Analysis:
Run the analyses that change how we operate, not just report the numbers. Construct the business case for process and systems investments. Tell the story.
